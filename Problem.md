# THE PROBLEM

- Every employee on Pepper's Customer Success team spends 4+ hours a week building reports for
clients.

## How would you solve this?

- A BIT OF CONTEXT
- Pepper Atlas is an AI-native platform that helps clients manage their organic and AI search visibility. CS
managers today pull data from a handful of sources, stitch it together in spreadsheets and decks, and
email it to clients on a recurring cadence.

## DATA AVAILABLE

- See the attached workbook: Data Available - Interview.xlsx. It documents endpoints across 7 sources
(GSC, GA4, Semrush, GEO, WordPress, Webflow, Contentful) each with inputs, outputs, and what
each field means.
- Treat them as black-box APIs — assume they return realistic data. No real calls required.
- You don't need every endpoint. Pick what you actually need.
- If your solution needs something not listed, say what you'd add and why.

## WHAT "DONE" LOOKS LIKE

- Working prototype - code, notebook, or agent that produces a real output (a report, a draft, a
recommendation).
- UX flow - a clickable artifact (Claude / Figma / HTML) showing the experience: who sees what,
when, and how.
- Eval / experiment design - how would you know if your solution actually saves the 4 hours? Define
the measurement, dataset, and success criteria.
- Product Requirement Doc - a document highlighting what the product is expected to do; a common
source of knowledge to be shared across teams for building the product in Atlas.


## CONSTRAINTS & NON-GOALS

- Skip auth, billing, and dashboard chrome - assume those exist.
- You can use any source.
- Focus more on competitor research and back any other assumptions with publicly available data.
